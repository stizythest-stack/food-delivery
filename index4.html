chicken

Chicken is a popular, high-protein food with varied nutritional profiles depending on the cut and preparation. While it offers significant health benefits, certain risks are associated with its consumption, mainly tied to unhealthy cooking methods, food safety, and processed products.
The nutritional content of chicken varies by cut. A standard 3.5-ounce (100-gram) serving of cooked, skinless chicken breast contains approximately: Calories: 165 Protein: 31 grams Fat: 3.6 grams (mostly unsaturated) Carbohydrates and fiber: 0 grams Health benefits Excellent source of lean protein: Chicken is a complete protein, providing all nine essential amino acids needed for muscle repair, growth, and maintenance. This helps promote satiety and can aid in weight management. Rich in vitamins and minerals: Chicken contains vital nutrients such as B vitamins (including B6 and B12), iron, zinc, phosphorus, and selenium. These support healthy brain function, energy production, and immune system health. Supports bone health: The minerals phosphorus and calcium found in chicken help maintain strong, healthy bones and can help reduce the risk of osteoporosis. Promotes heart health: Lean cuts of skinless chicken are naturally low in saturated fat and cholesterol, making them a heart-healthy alternative to some red meats.
